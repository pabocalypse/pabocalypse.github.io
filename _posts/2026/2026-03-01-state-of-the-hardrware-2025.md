This is just a personal hardware tech review for over 2025. For stuff the
general tech that I use. These generally have very specific reasons to exist
when they are bought. A piece of tech like a computer though, should be able to
drop into another place in order to fulfil a roll that it wasn't initially
purchased for. This is why some of the technology is over spec'ed rather than
being what it should be to be on a budget.

## Gaming PC

The gaming PC was mainly purchased in December 2024 to be just a gaming PC
mainly. For specs at the time, it was:

* **CPU:** AMD Ryzen 9700x
* **GPU:** Reuse 3060 TI previously purchased
* **RAM:** 64GB DDR5
* **Storage:** 2x 2TB Samsung NVMe drives + 4TB Samsung SSD
  * Reusing a 4TB SSD to store games from the previous build.
* **Motherboard:** B650M based board.

This was when the 64GB RAM kit was at roughly £200. The same kit goes for around
£1000 currently. The reason at the time for the amount of RAM is that the rule
of thumb for it was changing to 32GB for gaming as 16GB is becoming troublesome,
so I doubled that. For myself, I am fortunate to be in a position where this was
not an issue on cost. Would I go to 64GB now? No. I'd probably have to live with
16GB of RAM now due to the crazy prices. At that point as well, I would have to
have considered staying on the older system - being a 4th generation Intel i7
machine. This was mainly because it had 32GB of RAM. The machine itself though
was starting to become an issue when games are “optimising” at launch when you
update the graphics drivers. I was getting impatient with this.

Later on in the year I swapped the GPU from a 3060 TI to a 5060 TI due the
amount of VRAM. This was less about a performance boost if any, and just about
VRAM. This was mainly because a game I played throughout the year was
complaining due to the 8GB of VRAM that the 3060 TI had.

## Basic Small PC

Instead of using the gaming PC all of the time, I have a basic NUC type PC (ASUS
PN50) that I use for day to day stuff like work, basic web browsing, etc... It
was originally purchased in 2021 and has a 500GB NVME, with 16GB of DDR4 RAM.
I've only recently been hitting the RAM limitation with the amount of chrome
tabs that I have open, so am upgrading to a Beelink SER8 with 64GB of DDR5 RAM
and 2x 1TB NVMe drives. This will be the new basic computer going into 2026.

The specs of the ASUS PN50 are:

- **CPU:** AMD Ryzen 7 4700U
- **GPU:** Integrated
- **RAM:** 16GB DDR4
- **Storage:** 500GB NVMe

## Laptop

The laptop I have been using is a Dell XPS 13 7390 from 2019. This has started
acting weirdly with a lot more freezes that I am used to. The specs of this
laptop are:

* **CPU:** Intel Core i7-10510U
* **GPU:** Integrated
* **RAM:** 16GB LPDDR3
* **Storage:** 512GB NVMe

As I am unsure of the cause of the freezes, and that I have had to replace the
battery and keyboard already. I decided that it was time for an upgrade over to
a frame.work laptop. This is more to do with that they have done the replaceable
parts aspect of a laptop, and this is something that I would like to see more of
in the space. The replacement of the keyboard on the Dell laptop showed me how
much a pain it is just to do some maintenance on it.

For what will happen to the XPS, it’ll either get added to a stack of laptops or
utilized as a backup computer. I am currently unsure which way round it’ll go as
it depends on if it continues to freeze up in Windows land or not.

## iPad

During one of the sales, I bought an iPad. Whatever the cheapest model is. This
was mainly to try and have a weaker device than a laptop so I can do more
focused stuff. This was prior to iPadOS 26 getting released and it becoming more
like a mac. Whilst I am yet to upgrade to that version of the OS, I know that
I'll have to at some point in order to keep it on a supported version of the OS
for getting apps.

In terms of that original plan for device, I think the main purpose of an iPad
for me now is either entertainment whilst travelling or focused writing. Joplin
even supports multiple profiles on iPads as well, meaning that I could also use
this in the office in order to write my journal thing instead of having to type
it at home later. The only thing I miss by using this method is the easily
ability to copy and paste commands, code snippets, etc… to the journal itself.
Since I want to be as candid as possible in what I write, this should only be
accessible by myself.

## Phone

My phone is the only part of my technology stack that hasn't been upgraded near

## NAS

A box I use for a NAS is also hopefully going to change. This will be from a
desktop to a UGREEN DXP4800 Plus. The initial goal will just to move over some
SMB/NFS shares, and the containers that I run. This would then become the main
storage. The desktop can then be more easily turned off, and used as longer term
storage at a later time. The only downside in going from a desktop tower to one
of these prebuilt NASs is the noise as far as I can tell. I am going to have to
do some testing around this to make sure that it doesn’t adversely do anything.
Sadly the only drives I have that I can use for the tests are 5400RPM based
rather than 7200RPM based. This will have a slight impact on the test data,
however I don’t see this as being problematic. The last part of this is that I
generally encrypt the data as well at rest, so having a solution for that in the
prebuilt system will also be a requirement.
